The Last Song

2010

Director: Julie Anne Robinson

Screenplay: Nicholas Sparks & Jeff Van Wie

Cast: Miley Cyrus, Liam Hemsworth, Greg Kinnear, Bobby Coleman, Hallock Beals

Veronica "Ronnie" Miller is a seventeen-year-old whose life takes a dramatic turn after her parents' divorce. Her father relocates from New York City to Wilmington, North Carolina, causing Ronnie to harbor resentment and estrangement towards him. However, her perspective shifts when her mother decides it would be beneficial for Ronnie to spend the summer with her father in Wilmington.

In Wilmington, Ronnie's father, a former concert pianist and teacher, leads a quiet life immersed in creating a significant artwork for a local church. Through this experience, Ronnie embarks on a journey of self-discovery and reconciliation with her father. Along the way, she encounters various forms of love, including first love and the complex dynamics between parents and children.

As the story unfolds, Nicholas Sparks explores the intricate and often tumultuous nature of relationships, showcasing how they can both break and heal our hearts. Through poignant storytelling, the novel delves into themes of forgiveness, redemption, and the transformative power of love.
