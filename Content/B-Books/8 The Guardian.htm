The Guardian

2003

Julie Barenson's late husband left her two unexpected gifts: a puppy named Singer and the assurance that he would always watch over her. Now, four years later, Julie, at twenty-nine, feels too young to give up on love. She considers opening her heart again, but she faces a dilemma: should she choose Richard Franklin, a sophisticated and charming engineer who treats her like royalty, or Mike Harris, her husband's loyal best friend and a down-to-earth nice guy?

Julie believes that choosing one of them will bring her the happiness she has been longing for. However, her life takes a terrifying turn when she becomes ensnared in a nightmare fueled by deception and jealousy so toxic that it turns deadly.

In "The Guardian," Nicholas Sparks delivers his trademark qualities, but with an added layer of electrifying intensity and riveting suspense.
